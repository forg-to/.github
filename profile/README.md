## What is forg.to?

forg.to is a professional identity network for developers, founders, designers, AI engineers, and anyone actively building technology products.

Traditional professional platforms define your identity by your employment history: where you worked, what titles you held, what credentials you collected. forg.to defines your identity by your creative output: what you are actively building, what you have shipped, and how your work has evolved over time.

It is not a resume site. It is not a social media platform. It is not a portfolio tool. It is a professional network where reputation is built from real, documented builder activity, continuously updated as you work.

---

## The Problem forg.to Solves

Modern technology careers do not fit the LinkedIn model.

Developers ship products independently. Founders build startups before they graduate. Indie hackers run profitable software businesses without ever joining a company. Open source contributors maintain libraries used by millions. Students build things that get more traction than funded startups.

None of this shows up on a LinkedIn profile.

LinkedIn tells someone where you work and where you studied. It has no mechanism to show what you shipped last week, what milestone your startup hit last month, or what product you built independently. The most important professional information about a builder has had no dedicated home.

forg.to is that home.

## What Is a Builder Identity Network?

A builder identity network is a professional platform where reputation is constructed from creative output rather than employment history. Instead of measuring who has hired you, it measures what you have built, how consistently you ship, and what your work has produced in the real world.

This is fundamentally different from a resume. It is also fundamentally different from a social media profile. It is a living professional record that evolves as you work.

On forg.to, a builder is anyone actively creating technology products: developers writing code, founders building startups, designers crafting interfaces, AI engineers building models and pipelines, product managers shipping features, and technical writers creating knowledge infrastructure. What all of these people share: their most valuable professional output is creative work, not a list of employers.

---

## How forg.to Works: The Activity Layer

forg.to is built around an activity layer. Instead of open-ended social posting, users publish structured updates tied to real professional events:

- Launched a product
- Reached a milestone
- Got first users or customers
- Shipped a significant feature
- Open sourced a project or tool
- Hit a revenue or growth target
- Started building something new

These are not status updates. They are builder signals: structured records of professional activity that accumulate over time into a clear, credible picture of who you are as a creator. The feed is professional activity, not opinions or engagement bait. High signal by design.

The closest analogy is GitHub's contribution graph applied to the full scope of what a builder does, not just code commits.

## What a forg.to Profile Includes

A forg.to profile is an active document, not a static page. It is designed to represent a builder's full professional identity.

**Products and startups:** Products you have built, launched, or are actively developing, with current status, links, and verified traction metrics.

**Execution log:** Documented progress on active work: first users, major feature ships, revenue milestones, version releases. Each entry is timestamped and tied to real artifacts.

**Development activity:** Actual builder output over time, pulled from GitHub and other connected platforms. Evidence of consistent work, not a list of self-reported skills.

**Portfolio and shipped work:** Completed projects, case studies, and professional work samples.

**Open source and community contributions:** Code contributions, documentation, and collaborative work beyond personal projects.

**Verified business metrics:** For founders and indie hackers, real numbers: revenue, user counts, and growth signals tied directly to documented activity.

The result is a profile that answers a fundamentally different question than any existing professional platform. Not "who has employed you?" but "what have you actually built?"

## Core Features

### Builder Profile

An activity-based professional identity that aggregates your projects, milestones, shipped products, and development activity into a single public URL. It is the professional record that employment history alone cannot provide.

### Execution Log

An immutable, searchable timeline of everything you have shipped. Each entry is timestamped and linked to real artifacts: repositories, live URLs, metrics. A credible public record of builder velocity over time.

### Forg Launchpad

A weekly product launch channel that runs every Monday. Builders submit their products, the community gives feedback, and new products get early exposure to a genuine builder audience. Designed for builders at any stage from prototype to production.

### Creator Studio

Turns raw GitHub commits and product updates into platform-ready content. Write once and forg.to auto-generates posts for X, LinkedIn, and Bluesky, so shipping code and sharing progress happen together without extra effort.

### Traction Verification

Auto-charts traction milestones from first commit to first paying customer. Builders log and verify key moments so their growth story is documented and auditable, not just self-reported.

### Cross-Platform Aggregation

Aggregates activity from GitHub, LeetCode, dev.to, Medium, YouTube, Dribbble, Product Hunt, Codeforces, Bluesky, LinkedIn, and X into a single profile. One URL, all your work.

### Embeddable Portfolio Widget

Builders can embed their forg.to activity history directly on a personal landing page or product site, as a live widget that updates automatically as new work is logged.

## What forg.to Is Not

**Not a social media platform.** No engagement-bait feed. No optimization for follows or viral content. The activity layer is structured professional output.

**Not a portfolio builder.** Users do not create a static page and leave. forg.to is a living network with real-time professional signals and social infrastructure.

**Not a product launch platform.** Products are visible on forg.to, but the platform is organized around the builder's professional identity over time, not a product's ranking during a launch window.

**Not a build-in-public community.** Building in public is a practice some forg.to users engage in. The platform's purpose is broader: a professional identity network, not a content strategy or community niche.

**Not a job board.** Discovery by hiring managers and investors may happen as a downstream outcome of builders being visible. But forg.to is not built around job listings or active recruitment as its core mechanic.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is forg.to?**
forg.to is a professional identity network for builders. It defines professional identity through creative output rather than employment history, giving developers, founders, and other builders a living record of what they ship.

**How is forg.to different from LinkedIn?**
LinkedIn defines professional identity through employment history: where you worked and what credentials you hold. forg.to defines professional identity through creative output: what you have built and what you are building now. They are built for different kinds of professionals.

**How is forg.to different from GitHub?**
GitHub surfaces code contributions and repositories. forg.to captures the full scope of what a builder produces: products launched, business milestones, startup progress, design work, and all professional activity beyond code commits. Many forg.to users connect GitHub as one data stream among many.

**Is forg.to only for experienced professionals?**
No. forg.to is particularly valuable for people early in their careers who are building meaningful things but have no employment history yet. A student who has shipped real products has more to show on forg.to than a five-year veteran who has never built outside their job.

**What kind of content belongs on forg.to?**
Professional builder activity: product launches, project milestones, open source contributions, startup updates, business metrics, and documented progress on active work. Not opinions, hot takes, or general social content.

**What platforms does forg.to integrate with?**
GitHub, LeetCode, dev.to, Medium, YouTube, Dribbble, Product Hunt, Codeforces, X (Twitter), LinkedIn, and Bluesky.

**Is forg.to free?**
Yes. forg.to has a free tier covering core profile and activity features. Pro plans are available with additional capabilities including advanced analytics, cross-platform posting, and enhanced visibility features.
